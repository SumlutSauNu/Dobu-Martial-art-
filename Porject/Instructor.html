Kru Jason Fenton
Jason Fenton Director of Operations and Muaythai Development Jason officially became a Partner in Traditional Muaythai in January 2020. He is our school’s resident expert when it comes to all things Muaythai. He certifies our trainers to ensure the teaching styles and techniques are true to the art. Here is some background on Kru Jason’s history in Muaythai: Kru Jason started training Muaythai at the age of 15 in Calgary March of 1993 under the watchful eye of Ajarn Mike Miles. After only 6 months of training he stepped into the ring for the very first time. His fight career spanned over 15 years. Over the course of his fighting career Kru Jason was given many chances to not only represent his gym but also his country. Kru Jason has not only fought all across Canada but also throughout the United Sates as well as Mexico, the UK, Hungry and in several different cities in Thailand. Throughout the course of his 57-fight career, Kru Jason faced many of the best in the sport and even some Muaythai royalty.

Professor David Lee

Steve Nisewander
I started judo under Sensei Park, Sung Jae at Ball State University in September 1971. I took Judo as a PE class and Judo 2 the following term. While at the university I trained for over two years (1971-73). I was on the Ball State University Judo Team as the 154 lb competitor. At the end of this time I had achieved the rank of sankyu (brown belt) recognized by the U. S. Judo Federation. I was inducted into the U.S. Army in 1973.
